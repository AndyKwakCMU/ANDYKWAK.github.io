Part 2!

Soooo let's keep going with the ideas I brought up and refine it a little bit further. I threw in a lot of potential ways to tie the two different fields of music composition and probability together. However, the best way in terms of utilizing the idea of different cadences having different setups in terms of algorithmic trading would be a mix of Convolution Neural Network and Time-Series Sequence Model. Those are some big scary models that are hard to grasp conceptually, but if you think of it in terms of music, it might make a little bit more sense. CNN's are used primarily in pattern recognition, similar to how our ears instantly recognize the function of a cadence in any music. If we can hear the cadence, we can hear what came before it, hence recognizing the approach of a cadence. If the cadence is a breakout, and we can even deduce the direction of the breakout, we will most definitely be hearing music coming out of our mouths as we cop that guap. I was focusing on a markov chain implementation, it is essentially the same idea, except a markov chain would've been a terrible way to interface this algorithm on a large scale, there would be millions of nodes. We can still think of different chordal functions as states in the ticker data, which I think is a great interface for me to stare at while waiting for my algorithm to decimate my brokerage account.

The states can relate very closely with a Time-Series model as well. Since the Time-Series model is more "present" focused, and utilizes biases of recent relevant movement, our state can be much more than simply "we might make money" to "x% chance of maybe making money".
